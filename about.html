About

Hi, I am vdnamliv.

Security engineer, red teamer, occasional mentor, and forever curious about small repeatable habits.

Between client work and labs I keep this blog as a calm place to save ideas. The format is intentionally short: a few sketches about HackTheBox boxes, CTF recaps with runnable snippets, and quick checklists or scripts that saved me time.

I mentor a handful of students on basic security, computer networks, and competitive programming. Most of the notes that leak into the blog started as answers to their questions.

Current focus

  • Sharpening red team tradecraft without building huge tooling projects.
  • Capturing investigation flows so they can be replayed later.
  • Sharing approachable exercises for newcomers to security.

Things you will find here

  • Sketchy walkthroughs for HackTheBox with just enough command context.
  • Not-too-formal CTF writeups that highlight payloads and automation hints.
  • Tiny scripts and checklists that keep messy investigations tidy.
