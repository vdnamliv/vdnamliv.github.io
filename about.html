About

Hi, I am vdnamliv.

A security engineer still figuring things out, digging through labs, and trying to build a solid foundation instead of chasing fancy titles.

I keep this blog mostly for myself — a place to store the things I don’t want to forget: what worked, what broke, and the parts I misunderstood until the fifth attempt. Posts are short and practical because that’s how I learn: one small experiment at a time.

I’m currently sharpening my red team skills, understanding systems more deeply, and cleaning up my investigation habits so I stop repeating the same mistakes. I’m also putting effort into explaining concepts in a way my past self would’ve understood, especially for people who are just starting out.

What you’ll find here

  • Walkthroughs of labs (HTB, PortSwigger) with only the essentials — no fluff, no pretending.
  • CTF notes that focus on the actual thinking process, not polished stories.
  • Tiny scripts, checklists, and debugging steps that saved me from wasting hours.
  • Occasional reflections on learning security without a perfect background.
